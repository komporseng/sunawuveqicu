Suranne Jones Bio (Wiki)

Who is Suranne Jones?

Sarah Anne Akers better known as Suranne Jones is one of the well-known English actress and producer.

She is popular for her works in Doctor Foster: A Woman Scorned (2015), Scott & Bailey (2011), and Coronation Street (1960).

Who are the parents of Suranne Jones?

Suranne was born on 27 August 1978 in Oldham, Greater Manchester, England, UK. She grew up in a house on Foxdenton Lane, surrounded by two farms and their fields with her older brother, Gary Jones.

Her father’s name is Christopher Jones he is an engineer. Likely, the mother’s name is Jenifer Jones who was a secretary. Her mother passed away in the year 2016.

Suranne Jones was brought up a Catholic. Her priest advised her father Jones be christened Sarah Anne, instead of Jones. But her great-grandmother’s denied it telling Suranne would not be a proper name.

In which school did Suranne Jones attend?

Jones has her schooling at Cardinal Langley Roman Catholic High School in Middleton.

Suranne Jones is also a member of the Oldham Theatre Workshop. She obtained a BTEC National Diploma in Performing Arts at the Manchester School of Acting.

Suranne Jones: Career, Professional life

Acting

Suranne Jones started her career debuting a small role in television Coronation Street. She appears as Mandy Phillips, a girlfriend of Chris Collins.

She left the show in May 2004 after four years and the show and her role have gained her much recognition.

Jones then was cast in the television advertisement for Maltesers.  No sooner, she gained fame appearing as a guest in episodes of the series City Central and also played a role in My Wonderful Life.

Then, Suranne Jones made her appearances in Vincent,  Strictly Confidential, A Few Good Men, Harley Street (2008), and The Sarah Jane Adventures. Suranne appeared as Charity Dingle on the soap opera Emmerdale.

In January 2009, she played Ruth Slater in a three-part British television drama series, Unforgiven on ITV1. On New Year’s Day 2007, she performed in the Yorkshire and London-based black comedy, Dead Clever with Dean Lennox Kelly and Helen Baxendale on ITV1.

Her other notable roles are in Five Days (2010), Single Father (2010), Doctor Who (May 2011), The Secret of Crickley Hall (2012), Doctor Foster (2015), and Gentleman Jack (2019).

In February 2018, Suranne Jones played the role of the mother of a missing child in the Sky Atlantic drama Save Me.

Others

Suranne Jones has modeled for FHM and Loaded too. Jones has also filmed for BBC and HBO co-production Gentleman Jack.

Collaborating with the Half Moon Theatre on 1st Nov 2019, BBC Children In Need album Got It Covered releases its cover song Symphony. Jones was also a part of it providing vocals on their album’s cover track “It Must Be Love“.

Jones also works for numerous charitable organizations. She said that during her teenage she used to be part of various breast cancer campaigns along with her mother. Her mother was also diagnosed with breast cancer.

Suranne Jones also has worked with Christian Aid, traveling to Sierra Leone. Further, she worked with the Democratic Republic of Congo involved in different HIV concerns, women’s rights, and child soldiers’ projects.

Awards

The actress was voted Best Supporting Actress by readers of Doctor Who Magazine for her role in The Doctor’s Wife. She is one of 5 Coronation Street actresses to have won a Best Actress Award at the British Soap Awards.

In addition, Suranne Jones received the Theatregoers’ Choice Award in 2005. For the work in Scott& Balley, she received the ‘Royal Television Society Regional Award for Best Performance in a Single Drama or Drama series’.

Furthermore, the actress has received various nominations and won numerous of it.
